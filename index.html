Fish Simulation Game

An innovative koi fish simulation where a single fish model is transformed dynamically using custom shaders to generate unique colors and body features. Each fish is driven by genetic traits, determining its appearance and behavior. These genes are passed to the next generation, introducing slight mutations to ensure siblings share familial similarities while maintaining individuality. This blend of genetics and shader technology creates a rich, lifelike ecosystem where no two koi fish are exactly alike. This advanced koi fish simulation features a multi-layered shader system that creates highly detailed and dynamic fish models. The fish is composed of five layers of shaders/materials to achieve realistic and customizable appearances:

  • Base Layer Shader – Controls the fish’s body color, which is influenced by factors such as age, health, and genetics. The color adapts over time, offering a realistic representation of the fish’s life cycle.
  • Three Layers of Pigment Shaders – These layers project a custom pigment sheet onto the fish model, enabling intricate color patterns and variations. Players can adjust pigment properties, adding complexity and uniqueness to each fish.
  • Final Shader Layer – This layer handles the scales, metallic sheen, and other fine details, enhancing the fish’s realism with lifelike textures and lighting effects, creating a visually striking appearance.

    • Each of these shaders was created by me using Shader Graph, enabling a flexible and powerful system for creating realistic, customizable fish.

    Space Shooter

    Created during a one-day hackathon, this game combines typing practice with an action-packed space shooter. Players control a spaceship flying through space, typing words displayed on the screen. Each keypress triggers a shot that destroys approaching obstacles. The challenge lies in typing quickly to eliminate obstacles before they reach and collide with the ship, making it both a fun and educational typing speed exercise.
